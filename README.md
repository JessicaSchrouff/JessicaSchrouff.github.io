# Jessica Schrouff

### Biography
I studied Biomedical Engineering at the University of Liège, Belgium, where I then obtained my PhD. My work investigated the application of machine learning techniques to brain imaging data, for both clinical and cognitive neuroscience. I pursued this project as a post-doctoral fellow at Stanford University, CA, USA for four years and at University College London, UK for one year. I am now a Machine Learning Research Scientist at Prodo, a company providing automated code correction and generation.

### Output
My research has produced a variety of outputs, including scientific articles and book chapters ([Google Scholar profile](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=2YWm2nMAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao)), as well as software and code. My biggest project in this regard is the Pattern Recognition for Neuroimaging Toolbox [PRoNTo](http://www.mlnl.cs.ucl.ac.uk/pronto/).

### Other interests
I enjoy reading and hiking. I have plans for a [trip around the world](www.monkey-on-the-road.com) and am very interested in (gender) bias in humans (I created a Repository of Women in Neuroscience, [WiNRepo](www.winrepo.org)) and machines.
